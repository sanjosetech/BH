The Binghill Story

The story behind a brand that makes oversized insulated drinkware for real adventurers — not showroom warriors.

Chapter 01

It Started on a Mountain

The idea for Binghill came from a simple frustration: the gear we relied on for real adventure was either boring, fragile, or way too small. We needed drinkware that could keep up — big enough for a full day on the trail, cold enough to actually taste refreshing after hours in the sun, tough enough to survive being tossed in and out of a truck bed a thousand times.

So we built it ourselves.

Chapter 02

Two Years of Real Testing

We spent two years in workshops and in the field, working with metallurgists, outdoor athletes, and everyday adventurers — people who push their gear because they push themselves. We tested everything. We broke things. We rebuilt them better.

What emerged was a line of oversized insulated drinkware and coolers designed for real life — not the showroom, not the Influencer photo op, but the actual unpredictable, beautiful chaos of getting outside and living.

We don't see a contradiction between natural exploration and modern engineering. We see a responsibility.
Chapter 03

Nature and Innovation, Together

Binghill products are built from BPA-free, food-grade stainless steel — materials that last for years, not weeks. Every seal is tested for leak resistance. Every cooler is verified for 24-hour ice retention in real conditions.

We don't cut corners; we cut weight, because you have enough to carry. We believe the best gear doesn't just perform — it respects the places you take it.

Chapter 04

What We Make

50-ounce trail bottles. 64-ounce tumblers. 5-gallon ice buckets that actually keep ice frozen all day. Products with the capacity, durability, and design to match the people who use them.

No decorative curves. No compromise on function.
